Studiva — Internal Brief for New Team Members

1. What is Studiva
   Studiva is a centralized academic platform where students can quickly access structured study resources.

Core content types:

- Notes
- Chapter Summaries
- Question Banks
- Organizer (New Edition – key feature)
- Quizzes
- Flashcards
- Mock Tests
- Playlists

Purpose:
To eliminate scattered and unorganized study material and provide a searchable, structured system for exam preparation.

2. Core Model (How It Works)

Creators

- Users who upload study materials
- They can monetize their content
- They are the supply side of the platform

Students

- Users who consume content
- They are the demand side

Key dependency:
No creators → no content
No users → no value

3. Creator Strategy (Critical)

Positioning

- Focus on earning opportunity
- Keep income details intentionally vague
- Do not commit to specific numbers

Messaging

- “You can earn from your notes/resources”
- “Your content helps others and generates returns”

Important Rule
Never disclose or estimate earnings. The system is still evolving.

4. Why Students Will Use Studiva

Problems with Existing Solutions

- Telegram channels can get banned → content loss
- WhatsApp groups are cluttered → poor discoverability
- Platforms like Makaut Minds:
  - Too many groups
  - Repetitive messages
  - Important content gets buried

Studiva Advantage

- Tagged content (subject code, subject name, etc.)
- Search-based retrieval
- Organized repository (not chat-based)

Positioning line:
“Find what you need instantly, instead of scrolling endlessly.”

5. Content Scope
   Not limited to notes.

Post anything useful for students:

- Lab reports
- Assignments
- Practical guides
- Exam preparation material

Guideline:
If it can help a student academically, it belongs on Studiva.
